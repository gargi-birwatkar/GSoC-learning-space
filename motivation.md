## **Motivation**

### **Who I Am**
I am Gargi Birwatkar, a second-year Computer Engineering student and an aspiring software developer dedicated to building robust systems and AI models. I thrive on "hardcore" coding; the sheer difficulty of a problem is what provides the adrenaline rush I need to solve it. 

I love building complex systems. I start with a clear vision of the end goal and relentlessly iterate until I achieve perfection. My journey has taken me from developing an **Educative AR game for disaster management precaution training** (learning abstract concepts from scratch) to building an [**AI-Recipe Generator**](https://github.com/gargi-birwatkar/recipe-genie-multilang-ai) using Gemini. ### **Why Mesa?**
I am a deeply visual and creative person. I believe that if you can’t visualize a concept, you don't truly understand it. This philosophy led me to self-learn **Manim** to create a 2-minute [BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor)](https://github.com/gargi-birwatkar/BJT-Common-Emitter-Characteristics-Manim/tree/master) animation—a project that helped both me and my classmates grasp a difficult hardware concept.I developed few other [basic visualizations](https://github.com/gargi-birwatkar/Interactive-CS-Concepts) as well.


When I discovered **Mesa** during my GSoC research, it felt like a perfect alignment. Mesa offers the unique opportunity to bridge the gap between complex simulations and visual understanding. I want to contribute by creating easy-to-implement modules that empower students and teachers to build beautiful, high-impact simulations.

## What I want to learn

I want to shift my perspective from being a user to being the **architect**. Through this journey, I aim to master:
*   **System Scalability:** Understanding traffic management, concurrency control, and fault tolerance within simulation modules.
*   **Optimization:** Deepening my knowledge of rate-limiting, handling 426 errors, and making critical architectural decisions regarding parallel vs. non-parallel processing.
*   **AI Integration:** Gaining a more profound understanding of LLMs to make simulations more intuitive and visually appealing.
*   **Modular Design:** Building accessible, plug-and-play components that simplify complex tasks for the wider community.
